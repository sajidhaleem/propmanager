# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sajid (owner) and a small staff (manager, front-desk) of the 52A guesthouse in Lahore, Pakistan. They use the app daily on desktop to log bookings as guests arrive, track money in/out, and file guest identities with the Hotel Eye government portal. Currency is PKR; timezone is PKT. Not designers or power users — they need the day's state at a glance and fast data entry.

## Product Purpose

A private property-management system for a 4-room guesthouse: bookings (with CNIC capture and Hotel Eye compliance push), calendar occupancy, income/expenses/payouts, and monthly P&L reports. Success = a booking is logged in under a minute, today's arrivals/departures are obvious the moment the dashboard opens, and monthly numbers are trusted.

## Brand Personality

Calm, efficient, trustworthy. A quiet daily work tool — never flashy. Numbers and statuses read at a glance; the interface stays out of the way of the job.

## Anti-references

- Loud SaaS dashboards with gradient heroes and oversized vanity metrics
- Cluttered admin templates (Metronic-style) with ten widgets competing per screen
- Anything requiring hover-hunting or hidden gestures — staff must find every action directly

## Design Principles

1. **Today first** — the current day's arrivals, departures, and money owed lead every screen.
2. **Legible over dense** — larger, high-contrast text beats fitting more rows (owner explicitly asked for bigger fonts).
3. **One-tap truthful status** — booking/payment/Hotel Eye states are color-coded consistently everywhere and editable in place.
4. **No dead ends** — every empty state says what to do next; every error says how to fix it.
5. **Fast entry, safe edits** — creation is quick with smart defaults; destructive actions confirm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Prioritize readable type sizes (≥12px minimum anywhere, ≥14px for data) and ≥4.5:1 contrast for body text.
- Reduced-motion support (already implemented) must be preserved.
- Desktop-first, but core flows (view bookings, change status) must work on a phone.
